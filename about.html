Beginning of a Journey

My journey started in the quiet corners of university libraries and the flour-dusted kitchen of my student apartment. As a student, I spent my days lost in textbooks and my nights lost in novels. I was always searching for a sense of belonging, a way to connect the stories I read to the world I lived in. Between lectures, I found my escape in baking—the rhythmic kneading of dough became my meditation, a way to create something tangible in a world of abstract ideas. One rainy afternoon, tucked away in the linguistics section of the library, I came across a word that stopped me mid-page: Komorebi.

The Meaning Behind

Komorebi (木漏れ日)

The name of our humble bakery came from the Japanese word, "Komorebi." It describes the phenomenon of sunlight filtering through the leaves of trees. While seemingly simple, the term encapsulates not only a visual experience but also the feelings of serenity, transience, and connection with nature that accompany it.
For me, Komorebi represents warmth — the quiet comfort of gentle light, the kind that makes you pause, breathe, and appreciate the present moment. I wanted our bakery to embody that same feeling. Just like sunlight peeking through the trees brings calm and happiness, we hope that every visit to our bakery gives our customers a sense of peace, joy, and simple contentment.
